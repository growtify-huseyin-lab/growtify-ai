On June 23, Anthropic announced "Claude Tag": Claude now joins a Slack channel like a team member. You assign work by tagging @Claude; it breaks the task into stages, learns from the channel's context, and carries it out on its own while you focus on other things.

## In brief

- **Multiplayer:** one Claude per channel works with everyone; one person can pick up where another left off, and private work runs over DM.
- **Learns and acts proactively:** it learns from permitted channels and data sources, and surfaces what it "thinks you need to know" on its own.
- **Async + granular control:** it can spread tasks over days and set its own schedule; admins define per-channel permissions and separate Claude identities (a sales model can't see engineering data), with spend limits and logging.
- Available in beta for Claude Enterprise and Team, running on Opus 4.8, replacing the existing "Claude in Slack" app (30-day migration).

## Our take

The most striking detail is Anthropic's own figure: 65% of its product team's code is written by an internal version of Claude Tag. It shows how far the idea of moving AI out of a chat box and into where teams actually work — Slack, their tools, their code — can go. The real shift isn't the interface but the location: Claude as a member of the channel, not a tab you switch to.
