Google DeepMind and A24 have announced what they call a "first-of-its-kind" research partnership for filmmaking. Built on an investment by Google in A24, the collaboration puts A24's filmmakers and DeepMind's researchers at the same table to test, iterate, and build new creative workflows together. The core idea is clear: the tools are designed to expand a filmmaker's vision, not replace it.

## The details

- A multi-project R&D collaboration that evolves over time, with goals and technical outputs maturing as the work progresses.
- A24's teams will trial new techniques inside their own production pipelines, giving DeepMind real-world feedback.
- As DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis put it: "This hands-on collaboration provides Google DeepMind with invaluable feedback and guidance from leading artists."
- The stated aim is to "ensure the tools of the future are shaped by the creators who use them."

## Why it matters

Hollywood has long swung between suing AI companies and working with them. This partnership tackles the most sensitive part of that debate — the fear of replacing artists — by keeping the tool under the creator's control.

The real question isn't the technology itself, but how it's positioned: putting AI *alongside* the person doing the work, not *in place* of them. Whatever your industry, that's exactly the most important call you'll make when bringing AI into your own work.
