Google DeepMind and independent film studio A24 have announced what they call a "first-of-its-kind" multi-project research partnership. Built on a Google investment in A24, the deal has filmmakers building new creative workflows alongside DeepMind's researchers — with a clear idea: the tools are meant to expand a director's vision, not change it.

## In brief

- The deal is "a deep research and development collaboration spanning multiple projects over time," with goals maturing as the work progresses.
- A24 gets access to cutting-edge tech and expanded storytelling; DeepMind gets "invaluable feedback and guidance from leading artists."
- Google invested in A24, but the amount wasn't disclosed — and no specific films or timeline were named.
- DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis frames the goal as ensuring "the tools of the future are shaped by the creators who use them."

## Our take

Hollywood keeps swinging between suing AI companies and partnering with them; this is one of the most notable cases of choosing the second path. What's interesting is that instead of building a product alone and saying "here, use it," an AI lab is bringing the artist in while it's still at the drawing board. It's a practical attempt to answer the most sensitive question AI faces in creative fields — does it replace the artist, or stand beside them.
