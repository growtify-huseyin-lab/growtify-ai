Released on June 9, Fable 5 was suspended three days later. Amazon researchers had shown the model could be pushed past its safeguards to find software vulnerabilities and demonstrate how to exploit them; the U.S. government treated that as a national-security risk and imposed an export control. Because Anthropic couldn't verify users' nationality in real time, it cut access for everyone. When the control was lifted on June 30, the model returned to global access on July 1.

## In brief

- **Timeline:** June 9 launch → June 12 export control and suspension → June 26 limited domestic Mythos 5 access → June 30 control lifted → July 1 Fable 5 redeployed globally.
- **Why it was pulled:** Amazon researchers showed Fable 5 could be pushed past its safeguards to find software vulnerabilities and demonstrate how to exploit them; the government called it a national-security concern.
- **New safeguard:** a classifier targeting that technique now blocks problematic requests in "over 99%" of cases (Anthropic's own figure) and answers them via Opus 4.8 instead. Side effect: more false positives on routine coding and debugging.
- **Access:** on the Claude Platform, Claude.ai, Claude Code, and Cowork. July 1–7, Pro/Max/Team/select Enterprise plans include Fable 5 for up to 50% of weekly limits; after July 7, via usage credits. Cloud access (AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Foundry) is still pending.

## Our take

The turning point wasn't Fable 5 coming back — it was watching a government pull it from the global market with a single order. The model isn't even a weapon; by Anthropic's own account, "many less capable models" could produce the same output — and it was still switched off. That makes it concrete: frontier AI models are now treated like chips or arms, a strategic export item. The industry's reflex was to band together — Anthropic, Amazon, Microsoft, and Google built a shared jailbreak-severity framework. Which leaves one question: next time a control lands, will the company cut everyone off again, or will the plumbing finally be ready?
